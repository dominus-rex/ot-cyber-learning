Cisco Introduction to Cybersecurity Notes
Overview

Cybersecurity is the protection of systems, networks, devices, and data from unauthorised access, attack, or damage.

Cybersecurity aims to:

protect confidentiality
maintain integrity
ensure availability

This is commonly called the CIA triad.

Types of cyber threats
Malware

Malware is malicious software designed to damage or gain access to systems.

Examples:

viruses
worms
ransomware
trojans
spyware
Phishing

Phishing attacks attempt to trick users into revealing passwords or sensitive information through fake emails or websites.

Social engineering

Social engineering targets people rather than technology.

Examples:

impersonation
fake support calls
phishing emails
tailgating
Denial of Service

A Denial of Service attack attempts to overload systems or networks to make services unavailable.

CIA Triad
Confidentiality

Protecting data from unauthorised access.

Examples:

passwords
encryption
access control
Integrity

Ensuring data is accurate and not modified without permission.

Examples:

hashing
file permissions
change control
Availability

Ensuring systems and services remain accessible.

Examples:

backups
redundancy
disaster recovery
Common security devices
Firewall

Controls network traffic entering and leaving a network.

Antivirus

Detects and removes malicious software.

Intrusion Detection System

Monitors networks for suspicious activity.
